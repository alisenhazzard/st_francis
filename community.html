Parish Mission

St. Francis of Assisi Catholic Church is a Christ-centered community. We strive to be a warm, friendly and caring parish family, where the gifts and talents of all, young and old, are recognized and graciously used to nurture others, and to worship together in a vibrant and spirit-filled liturgy. We will go forth healed, affirmed and refreshed to meet the challenge of life in our spiritual growth.

Women's Guild

The Women's Guild is under the spiritual guidance of Mary, Our Lady of Good Council, who is the Patroness of the National Council of Catholic Women. The purpose of the Women's Guild is:

  • To unite all women of our parish in religious, educational, and social activities.
  • To assist Father Fitzgerald in spiritual and material undertakings for the welfare of the church.
  • To serve as the agency through which women of the church participate in the Diocesan program of Catholic action by affiliation with the East Hillsborough Deanery and the Diocesan and National Council of Catholic Women.

Past Guild activities have included an annual retreat for all women of the parish, a Halloween party for children, and a St. Patrick's Day dance for the parish. We also have Masses for deceased and sick members and their relatives, as well as and Evening of Reflection. We make charitable donations to Everyday Blessings (a program for abused and neglected children), Priestly Formation, San Jose Mission, Morning Star, and Vacation Bible School.

The Women's Guild meets at 7:30pm on the first Tuesday of each month between September and May. For more information, please contact Women's Guild President, Theresa Redgrave at (813) 926-9808.

Knights of Columbus

The Knights of Columbus is a Catholic men's fraternal benefit society that was formed to render financial aid to members and their families. Mutual aid and assistance are offered to sick, disabled, and needy members and their families. The Order is based upon the principles of Charity, Unity, Fraternity, and Patriotism and provides members and their families the opportunity to serve in activities and programs supporting the church and the community.

Please visit our website for more information about becomming involved.

Legion of Mary

The Legion of Mary welcomes all active Catholics interested in offering their services for spiritual work as needed by our pastor, Father Fitzgerald. Under the banner of Mary and with her help we endeavor to become holy in our own lives and spread a deeper devotion to her.

The Legion was founded by Frank Duff and began in Dublin, Ireland in 1921. It now encompasses more than three million members in nearly one thousand dioceses throughout the world. The first Praesidium (parish unit) was founded in the United States in 1931. At St. Francis of Assisi, Our Lady Queen of Peace of Praesidium was founded in 1987. We currently have 19 active members and 95 auxiliary members.

Our objectives include:

  • Personal sanctification of our members by taking Mary as our model, and endeavoring to imitate her virtues, especially her faith, charity, and humility.
  • Spread devotion to Mary, primarily by encouragin the recitation of the Rosary, as Mary requested at Fatima.
  • Performance of apostolic work under the spiritual direction of the parish unit by Deacon Jerry Thomas.

The Legion meets every Tuesday at 8:45am after morning mass. For more information, please contact the Legion of Mary Parish Coordinator, Henri Plourde at (813) 689-3374.

RCIA and RCIC

  • Are you interested in learning more about the Catholic Church?
  • Are you a baptized Catholic who has been away from active membership in the church, and you wish to return?
  • Are you feeling that the Lord is calling or urging you to come to Him?
  • Are you concerned that your marriage would prohibiut your joining the church?

We invite you to come and be part of our RCIA (The Rite of Christian Initiation of Adults) or RCIC (The Rite of Christian Initiation of Children). The RCIA/RCIC journey begins in September. Please read the brief outline and complete the registration form. We will be glad to call and extend a personal invitation and to answer any of your questions.

We are committed to helping you on your journey and look forward to speaking with you!
